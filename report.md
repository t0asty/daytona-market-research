# Q1 marketing & conversion briefing

**Audience:** leadership (non-specialist) · **Period:** 2026 Q1 · **Data as of:** 28 March 2026

---

## Part 1 — What to know and do (~1 page)

### Bottom line

Demand for the brand in search is up, but **we are showing up less often** for those searches than before—so we risk handing ready-to-buy traffic to competitors. At the same time, **checkout on phones is leaking**: fewer people finish buying, and the slowest pages got slower. **Paid social** is still working for finding new customers, but **retargeting** (ads to people who already visited us) is getting more expensive and tired creatives are part of the story. **Concept B** clearly moved “would you consider us?” in tests—we should scale it carefully.

### Do these five things (concrete next steps)

| # | Action | Why it matters | Suggested owner |
|---|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Protect brand search this week** — raise bids or add budget on core brand terms until impression share recovers. | Brand searches are up ~18% week over week, but our **impression share** (share of ad slots we win) fell **5 points to 72%**. That gap is money left on the table—or taken by others. | Growth / paid search |
| 2 | **Fix mobile checkout validation** — simplify payment-details fields and errors; prioritize **Safari on mobile** where errors cluster. | **62% of checkout exits** happen on payment details; overall **checkout completion is 41%**, down **2.1 points**. | Product + eng |
| 3 | **Refresh retargeting creatives** — replace or rotate the top 3 static assets (they’re ~6 weeks old and dominate volume). | Retargeting **CPM** (cost per thousand impressions) is **+11% week over week**; those three assets are **45% of impressions** and **CTR** is **~19% below** account average. | Creative + paid social |
| 4 | **Shift ~10% of social budget** into the two prospecting ad sets that already beat CPA target by **~14%**. | Low effort scale where we’ve already proven efficiency. | Paid social |
| 5 | **Scale “Concept B” into always-on** with frequency caps and a rotation plan for hero assets. | **+9 points** lift in consideration vs holdout in geo test; long-run heroes need rotation to avoid fatigue. | Brand + media |

### If we deprioritize

**Brand search:** Demand we don’t capture; competitors benefit. **Checkout:** Friction and slower mobile pages compound the **−2.1 pt** completion slip. **Retargeting:** Same budget reaches fewer people as costs rise.

*(Jargon used above is defined in the appendix.)*

## Part 3 — Ranked recommendations (for planning)

Same priorities as the data pipeline; detail for teams who will scope work.

1. **Defend core brand terms before competitor conquest ramps** — High impact, low effort (priority **85**). Impression share down **5 pts** while generic CPC rose; brand still best ROAS. *(Sources: paid search · confidence ~0.77)*

2. **Refresh top-fatigued creatives in retargeting** — High impact, medium effort (**80**). Align search + social tests where the same “fatigue” theme appears. Social: three statics = **45%** of impressions, **~19%** CTR gap vs average. *(paid search, paid social · ~0.82)*

3. **Simplify checkout step validation on mobile** — High impact, medium effort (**78**). Majority of exits on payment details; Safari mobile over-indexes on validation errors. *(funnel · ~0.71)*

4. **Re-allocate ~10% budget to best-performing prospecting ad sets** — Medium impact, low effort (**65**). Two ad sets beat CPA target by **~14%** with headroom. *(paid social · ~0.82)*

5. **Scale concept B into always-on with guardrails** — Medium impact, low effort (**55**). Significant lift in two geos; cap frequency to limit saturation. *(creative · ~0.68)*

## Appendix — Terms

- **Impression share:** How often our ad is shown when it’s eligible (here, core brand searches).
- **CPM:** Cost per thousand impressions.
- **CTR:** Click-through rate.
- **LCP (mobile):** How long until main content is visible; higher seconds = feels slower.
- **Consideration lift:** Uplift in “would consider us” in test vs a holdout group.
- **ROAS:** Return on ad spend (revenue per dollar spent).

## Appendix — Method note

Mock / pipeline data assumes **last-click attribution**; incrementality is **not** modeled here. Use this doc as a structured brief, not a final incrementality read.
